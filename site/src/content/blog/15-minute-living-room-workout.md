Here's the truth nobody puts on a fitness poster: the best workout is the one you'll actually do. And on a busy morning, that's rarely a 90-minute gym session. It's fifteen minutes, in your living room, still half-awake.

So make your coffee, let it sit, and let's beat the clock. No equipment, no floor-is-too-cold excuses, no changing into "real" workout clothes.

![Quick living-room morning workout](/images/strong-s1.png)

## Why 15 Minutes Actually Works
Short workouts get unfairly dismissed. But moving with focus for fifteen minutes beats "I'll do an hour later" — which, let's be honest, becomes never. **Consistency crushes intensity** over time. A daily fifteen wins the year.

## The Warm-Up (2 minutes)
Don't skip this. Cold muscles are cranky muscles.
- **March in place** — 30 seconds
- **Arm circles** — 30 seconds
- **Gentle torso twists** — 30 seconds
- **Standing side bends** — 30 seconds

You should feel warmer, not tired. That's the goal.

## The Main Circuit (10 minutes)
Do each move for **40 seconds**, rest 20, then move on. Complete the circuit twice.

### 1. Squats
Sit back like there's a chair behind you. Knees track over toes. This is your legs' wake-up call.

### 2. Incline Push-Ups
Hands on the couch or coffee table. Easier than the floor, still works your whole upper body. No shame in the incline — it's smart, not soft.

### 3. Reverse Lunges
Step back, drop down, stand up. Alternate legs. Great for balance and cranky mornings.

### 4. Glute Bridges
On your back, feet flat, lift the hips. Your body's secret weapon against a day of sitting.

### 5. Standing Knee-to-Elbow
A little cardio, a little core, a lot of "okay, I'm awake now."

![Woman doing a bodyweight move at home](/images/strong-s2.png)

## The Cool-Down (3 minutes)
- **Forward fold** — let your head hang, 30 seconds
- **Chest stretch** in a doorway — 30 seconds
- **Quad stretch**, each side — 30 seconds
- **Big slow breaths** — 30 seconds

## The Payoff
That's it. Fifteen minutes, full body, zero equipment — and your coffee's probably *just* the right temperature now.

Do this three or four mornings a week and you'll feel the difference in two weeks: steadier energy, a stronger back, and the quiet pride of a promise kept before 8am. **Start tomorrow. Same time, same couch.**
